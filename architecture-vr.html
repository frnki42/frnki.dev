Project Overview

The Architecture VR Showcase represents a paradigm shift in architectural visualization. By leveraging the power of Unreal Engine 5 and the Meta Quest 3's advanced capabilities, this project enables architecture firms to offer their clients an unprecedented level of immersion in unbuilt spaces.

Unlike traditional 2D renderings or even 3D models on screens, VR walkthroughs allow clients to truly experience the scale, proportions, and atmosphere of their future spaces. They can walk through rooms, examine materials up close, and understand spatial relationships in a way that was previously impossible.

This project is currently in active development, with a focus on optimizing performance for standalone VR headsets while maintaining the high visual fidelity that architecture clients expect.

Key Features

Real-time Rendering

Leveraging Unreal Engine 5's Lumen technology for dynamic global illumination and realistic lighting conditions at any time of day.

Photorealistic Materials

High-resolution PBR materials and textures that accurately represent real-world surfaces, from polished marble to rough concrete.

Interactive Elements

Clients can interact with doors, windows, and lighting systems to experience how the space functions in different scenarios.

1:1 Scale Accuracy

Every measurement is precisely calibrated to ensure that the VR experience accurately represents the actual dimensions of the designed space.

Material Variants

Switch between different material options in real-time, allowing clients to compare finishes and make informed decisions.

Optimized Performance

Carefully optimized to run smoothly on Meta Quest 3 hardware while maintaining visual quality through advanced LOD systems.

Technical Implementation

Development Stack

  • • Engine: Unreal Engine 5.5 with Nanite virtualized geometry
  • • VR Framework: Meta XR SDK for Quest optimization
  • • Scripting: Blueprint visual scripting for rapid iteration
  • • Asset Pipeline: Datasmith for CAD to UE5 workflow
  • • Version Control: Perforce for large binary asset management

Performance Optimization

Achieving photorealistic quality on mobile VR hardware requires careful optimization:

  • • Aggressive LOD (Level of Detail) systems for complex geometry
  • • Baked lighting for static elements combined with dynamic lights where needed
  • • Texture atlasing and compression to minimize memory usage
  • • Occlusion culling to avoid rendering unseen geometry
  • • Fixed foveated rendering to concentrate detail where users look
